HISTORY OF OUR SCHOOL


J/Vembadi Girls’ High School summons a long chronicle with perceptible accomplishment among the schools in the Northern Province. It boasts of a long history with rich grandeur. Methodist Missionaries, who arrived from England, decided to start a school for girls. When we looking back at the precedent history, there were several past principals who rendered their dedicated service to the school intensely. The first principal was Mrs. Peter Percival who assumed duties in the year 1837. The first National Principal was Ms.Mabel Thambiah. J/Vembadi Girls’ High School was taken over by the government in 1960. In 1984 this school became a National School. The school underwent much difficulty during the years 1984-1996 due to the civil war It has lost its majestic buildings which stood as monuments of the rich heritage.

We can’t forget the period of Mrs.K.Ponnampalam (1996 –2011). She courageously rebuilt the school with the help of the Ministry of Education, parents, well-wishers and old girls. She labored hard to restore the school’s pristine glory. However, the hostel facility for the students is not available after the civil war and students from faraway places face difficulties due to this short coming. The fame has grown higher and higher in recent period under the stewardship of the present principal Mrs.V.Shanmukaratnam who took over her duty on 2.7.2012 in this institution. Two new buildings were constructed during the period 2013-2016 for the Technological Laboratory and Technological Faculty and handed over to the students’ activities in 2015 and 2016 respectively. At the same time we have lost the space for the playground as we took a part of the playground for the above construction works.

VGHS won the 1st place in the senior level in Governor’s Trophy English Debate Competition held in 2014 while the junior level team secured the 3rd place. Miss.Jeayard Joicy Payus Deneja won the first place in weight lifting competition in the National level in 2013 and 2014. She has won the award for the best weight lifter in girls (118Kg) - 2014. Tharanka Kugathas of our school has won the Silver Medal in National Mathematics Olympiad competition. She also won the Bronze Medal in All Island School Mathematics competition - 2016 Nitharshana Nirmaleswaran won the Bronze Medal in the all Island inter schools Cycle race in 2016.

Every year, the pass rate index seems to be 100% in G.C.E (O/L) and 70% - 80% in G.C.E.(A/L). Last year, in the G.C.E (O/L) examination Vembadi Girls’ High School obtained the 1st place among the Tamil medium. Our achievements recorded as 9A -57, 8A- 53, 7A -32 and 6A -16.In G.C.E (A/L) examination held in August 2016. Hamsha Thananjeyan got 3A and became 1st in district in Bio stream. She won a scholarship and had been to Japan.

Further, in the Advanced Level Examination held in August 2017 our student Kamaleswary Senthilnathan secured the 1st rank in District and became 3rd in the Island in the Technology stream. Currently, there are 2563 students enrolled and 126 staff toiling hard to provide better education and train the students to face challenges in their life. Associations, clubs and committees encourage the students to take part in co–curricular activities and bring fame to the school by winning awards and medals.
